# WSET Level 2 Award in Wines — Specification (Source Summary)

**Source:** `raw/articles/wset/wset-l2-wines-spec.md` (markdown rendering) + `…/wset-l2-wines-spec.pdf` (immutable original)
**Origin:** Wine & Spirit Education Trust, Issue 1.1, 2022 (© 2019, 2022). Qualification no. **603/4432/5**.
**Type:** Official awarding-body qualification specification (syllabus + exam regulations).

## What it is

The published specification (syllabus) for the **WSET Level 2 Award in Wines** — the second rung of the WSET wine ladder (L1 → **L2** → L3 → L4 Diploma). It defines the **learning outcomes the exam tests**, recommended tasting samples, exam format, and regulations. It is the authoritative statement of *what an L2 candidate must know*; it is not itself a textbook (the study text is *Wines: Looking Behind the Label*).

This complements the existing WSET **Level 1** notes ([[wine-types]], [[wine-serving-temperature]], [[wine-food-pairing]]) — same framework, materially deeper scope (named grape varieties, GIs/regions, and labelling terms by country).

## Key content

- **Six Learning Outcomes (LOs):** (1) vineyard/environmental influences & grape-growing; (2) winemaking & bottle ageing; (3) the principal grape varieties + their GIs; (4) regionally important grape varieties; (5) sparkling & fortified production; (6) storage, service & food pairing.
- **Exam weighting (50 MCQs):** LO1 = 5, LO2 = 4, **LO3 = 19, LO4 = 12**, LO5 = 6, LO6 = 4 — grape varieties & regions (LO3+LO4) are **62% of the exam**.
- **Principal grape varieties (LO3):** black — Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, Syrah/Shiraz, Pinot Noir; white — Chardonnay, Pinot Grigio/Gris, Riesling, Sauvignon Blanc. Each mapped to PGI/PDO GIs across France, Italy, Spain, Germany, USA, Chile, South Africa, Australia, New Zealand.
- **Regional varieties (LO4):** Gamay, Grenache/Garnacha, Tempranillo, Nebbiolo, Barbera, Sangiovese, Corvina, Montepulciano, Zinfandel/Primitivo, Pinotage, Carmenère, Malbec (black); Chenin Blanc, Sémillon, Viognier, Gewurztraminer, Verdicchio, Cortese, Garganega, Fiano, Albariño, Furmint (white) — each with its key GIs and labelling terms.
- **Labelling/GI framework:** EU PDO/PGI and the France (AOC/AOP/VdP/IGP), Italy (DOCG/DOC/IGT), Spain (DOCa/DO/VdlT), Germany (Qualitätswein/Prädikatswein/Landwein) hierarchies; quality terms (Crianza/Reserva/Gran Reserva, Classico/Riserva, Premier/Grand Cru, Kabinett→TBA, etc.).
- **Sparkling & fortified (LO5):** traditional vs tank method; Sherry (fortify after fermentation, flor/oxidative ageing) vs Port (fortify to interrupt fermentation); GIs (Champagne, Asti, Prosecco, Cava; Sherry, Port) and their labelling terms.
- **Service & pairing (LO6):** storage/preservation, service temperatures by style, faults (cork taint, oxidation/heat damage), and food↔wine interaction components.

## Exam facts

Closed-book, **50 MCQs in 60 min**, 1 mark each, no negative marking. **Pass = 55%**; Merit ≥ 70%; Distinction ≥ 85%. TQT 28 h (17 GLH + 11 private study). Offered only via Approved Programme Providers (APPs).

## Open questions / scope notes

- The spec lists *what* must be known but not the canonical tasting-note descriptors per variety — those live in the study text, not here. Variety flavour profiles in the WSET2 node are synthesised, traceable to the spec only at the "characteristics tracked" level.
