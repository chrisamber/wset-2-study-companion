# WSET Level 2 Award in Wines — Mock Exam

**Format (matches the real exam):** 50 multiple-choice questions · 60 minutes · one correct answer each · 1 mark per question · **no marks deducted for wrong answers** · pass = 55% (28/50). Merit ≥ 70% (35), Distinction ≥ 85% (43).

**Weighting of this paper (same as the real exam):** LO1 = Q1–5 · LO2 = Q6–9 · LO3 = Q10–28 · LO4 = Q29–40 · LO5 = Q41–46 · LO6 = Q47–50.

> **Integrity note:** Every question and answer is drawn from the WSET L2 specification (`raw/wset-l2-wines-spec.pdf`) and this pack's `raw/` files. No facts were invented; all distractors are real WSET L2 terms. Questions 7, 22, 26, 45 and 47 are WSET's own published sample questions. Answer key with the basis for each is at the bottom — don't scroll until you've finished.

## Questions

**1.** Tannins in wine come primarily from which parts of the grape?
a) The pulp and juice
b) The skins, seeds and stems
c) The water in the berry
d) The sugar in the pulp

**2.** Besides warmth, sunlight, water and nutrients, what else does a vine require to grow grapes?
a) Nitrogen gas
b) Oxygen
c) Carbon dioxide
d) Added sulfur dioxide

**3.** The change in grape colour that marks the start of ripening is called:
a) Flowering
b) Fruit set
c) Véraison
d) Malolactic conversion

**4.** As grapes ripen on the vine, what generally happens to their sugar and acidity?
a) Sugar falls and acidity rises
b) Sugar rises and acidity falls
c) Both sugar and acidity rise
d) Both sugar and acidity fall

**5.** The French quality category Appellation d'Origine Contrôlée (AOC/AOP) is the equivalent of which EU term?
a) Protected Geographical Indication (PGI)
b) Protected Designation of Origin (PDO)
c) Wine without geographical indication
d) Landwein

**6.** During alcoholic fermentation, yeast converts grape sugar into:
a) Alcohol and carbon dioxide
b) Acid and tannin
c) Water and oxygen
d) Colour and sweetness

**7.** Compared with red wines, white wines are generally fermented at:
a) Higher temperatures
b) The same temperature
c) Either higher or lower temperatures
d) Lower temperatures

**8.** Malolactic conversion softens a wine by changing:
a) Lactic acid into malic acid
b) Tart malic acid into softer lactic acid
c) Sugar into alcohol
d) Tannin into colour

**9.** Which vessel is most likely to add aromas of vanilla, spice and toast to a wine?
a) Stainless steel tank
b) Concrete vat
c) Old, neutral large cask
d) New oak barrel

**10.** The principal black grape of the Médoc and Pauillac (Left Bank Bordeaux) is:
a) Merlot
b) Cabernet Sauvignon
c) Pinot Noir
d) Syrah

**11.** A full-bodied red with high tannin and aromas of blackcurrant and cedar is most likely made from:
a) Pinot Noir
b) Gamay
c) Grenache
d) Cabernet Sauvignon

**12.** Saint-Émilion and Pomerol (Right Bank Bordeaux) are based mainly on which grape?
a) Cabernet Sauvignon
b) Merlot
c) Sauvignon Blanc
d) Syrah

**13.** Hermitage and Côte-Rôtie in the Northern Rhône are made from which black grape?
a) Grenache
b) Pinot Noir
c) Syrah
d) Nebbiolo

**14.** Australia's Barossa Valley is especially famous for which grape?
a) Shiraz
b) Pinot Noir
c) Chardonnay
d) Sangiovese

**15.** The red wines of Gevrey-Chambertin and Nuits-Saint-Georges in Burgundy are made from:
a) Merlot
b) Cabernet Sauvignon
c) Pinot Noir
d) Gamay

**16.** A light-bodied red with low tannin, high acidity and red cherry / earthy aromas is most likely:
a) Syrah
b) Pinot Noir
c) Malbec
d) Tempranillo

**17.** Central Otago in New Zealand is best known for which grape?
a) Sauvignon Blanc
b) Riesling
c) Pinot Noir

**18.** Chablis, Meursault and Puligny-Montrachet are all made from which grape?
a) Sauvignon Blanc

**19.** Which grape produces both crisp, unoaked Chablis and rich, oaked white Burgundy?
a) Chardonnay

**20.** Sancerre and Pouilly-Fumé in the Loire are made from which grape?
a) Chardonnay

a) In a pressurised tank

a) Chardonnay
b) Glera

a) Cava

**47.** What is the ideal service temperature for a sweet wine?
a) Room temperature
b) Lightly chilled
c) Chilled
d) Well chilled

**48.** A wine that smells musty, like wet cardboard or a damp cellar, is most likely affected by:
a) Heat damage
b) Cork taint (TCA)
c) Excess tannin
d) High alcohol

**49.** Wine bottles sealed with a natural cork are best stored:
a) Standing upright
b) On their side
c) Upside down
d) In direct light

a) Oily fish such as mackerel
b) A fresh green salad with vinaigrette
